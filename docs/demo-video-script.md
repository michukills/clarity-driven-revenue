# RGS Public Demo Video — Script & Visual Sequence

**Voice:** Calm, direct, honest, practical, owner-respecting, system-focused. A friend being honest with a small business owner. Not motivational. Not agency. Not AI-sounding.

**Length target:** 75–90 seconds.
**Aspect:** 16:9 for /demo embed. A 9:16 cut can be derived from the same script.
**On-camera or VO:** Either works. Designed to be readable as a calm voiceover over screen-recorded UI and motion graphics.

---

## Voice anchors (used throughout)
- Most owners are not failing from lack of effort.
- Busy is not the same as stable.
- The point is not to make the owner need RGS forever. The point is to make the business clearer.
- The Revenue Control System™ is continued guidance and visibility, not dependency.
- A gear usually does not fail all at once. One worn tooth starts slipping. Then the next part of the system has to carry pressure it was not built to carry.
- The 0–1000 Business Stability Scorecard is a system check, not a personality quiz or motivational assessment.

---

## Final script (read aloud or set as on-screen captions)

**[00:00 — Open. Quiet.]**
> Most small business owners are not failing from lack of effort.
> They are running a business that is not as stable as it looks from the outside.

**[00:08 — Why RGS exists.]**
> Busy is not the same as stable. Revenue can be moving while the system underneath it is slipping. Most owners feel it before they can name it.

**[00:18 — The gear metaphor.]**
> A gear usually does not fail all at once. One worn tooth starts slipping. Then the next part of the system has to carry pressure it was not built to carry. That is what most "sudden" business problems actually are.

**[00:30 — Scorecard.]**
> The 0–1000 Business Stability Scorecard is a system check, not a personality quiz or motivational assessment. It looks at the five places revenue usually breaks: demand, conversion, operations, financial visibility, and how much the business depends on the owner.

**[00:44 — Diagnostic.]**
> The Diagnostic goes deeper. It identifies what is actually breaking, where the system is slipping, and what needs attention first. Before you spend more money on the issue, it helps to know what is actually breaking.

**[00:57 — Implementation.]**
> Implementation turns that into a repair plan and installs clearer structure into how the business actually runs. Not theory. Not a binder. Just systems that match how the work actually happens.

**[01:08 — Revenue Control System™ + guided independence.]**
> After the work is done, the Revenue Control System™ keeps the important signals in front of the owner. The point is not to make you need RGS forever. The point is to make the business clearer — so decisions get easier when the right information is already visible.

**[01:22 — Close. Honest.]**
> No one opens a business with the intention of closing it. RGS is not here to think for the owner. It is here to make the business easier to think through.
> If you want to see where your business stands, start with the Scorecard.

**Motion direction:** slow, deliberate, no whooshes, no upbeat music. If music is added, instrumental, low BPM, low intensity — closer to ambient than corporate.

**Type:** Source Serif 4 for any line that lands (anchors), Inter for everything else. No emoji. No exclamation marks anywhere.

**Color usage:** Dark background (#1F1F1F). Primary green (#6B7B3A) for the highlighted gear tooth and the score. White/cream for body type. Never neon, gradients across the spectrum, or stock-startup purple.

## What is intentionally not in the script

- No "transform / unlock / scale / dominate / next level" language.
- No claim of revenue uplift or guaranteed outcomes.
- No "AI" branding, no robotic narration jokes.
- No agency-style "we've helped X owners…" social proof (saved for case-study content where it can be substantiated).
- No music that suggests a product launch ad.

If recorded as voiceover: read each line at a normal speaking pace with a small pause after every period. The pacing is the message.
