# First-Client Delivery Boundaries (P35)

This document is the single source of truth for what RGS delivers, what RGS
does not deliver, and how the first-client workflow runs end to end. It is
co-owned by sales copy, the public site, and admin operations.

RGS is an architect, not an operator. RGS diagnoses, designs, structures,
and guides system improvement. RGS does not become the client's outsourced
employee, ad manager, bookkeeper, salesperson, accountant, attorney, or
daily operator.

## Business Stability Diagnostic — $3,000

**Includes**
- Review of one primary product, service, or revenue path
- System-level assessment through the RGS Stability System (5 gears):
  Demand Generation, Revenue Conversion, Operational Efficiency,
  Financial Visibility, Owner Independence
- Review of submitted intake details, scorecard / diagnostic answers, and
  approved supporting information
- Evidence-backed findings where data is available
- Client-safe report
- Prioritized roadmap and next-step recommendations
- Plain explanation of what is likely slipping and why
- Optional review conversation if part of the agreed offer

**Does not include**
- Done-for-you advertising, social posting, or website building
- Bookkeeping, accounting, payroll
- Legal, tax, financial, HR, or compliance advice
- Hiring/firing decisions or daily operations management
- Guaranteed revenue, growth, or business outcomes
- Unlimited revisions or unlimited products/services
- Implementation execution or software development
- Connector troubleshooting beyond the diagnostic scope unless explicitly
  agreed

**Scope rule.** A standard Diagnostic covers one primary product, service,
or revenue path unless additional scope is agreed in writing. Multiple
offers, locations, or business lines may require an admin-created add-on
or custom offer.

## RGS System Implementation — starting at $10,000

**May include**
- Turning diagnostic findings into a structured implementation plan
- SOPs, operating standards, and system changes
- Recommended tools/templates and workflow design
- Accountability structure, prioritization, and owner decision support
- Guidance on installing the repair
- Revenue Control Center™ access during implementation
- 30-day post-implementation grace

**Does not mean**
- RGS becomes the client's employee or runs daily operations
- RGS manages ad accounts or posts content
- RGS performs bookkeeping or accounting
- RGS gives legal, tax, or financial advice
- RGS guarantees the fix will create revenue growth
- RGS executes every task hands-on unless separately contracted

Implementation is admin-offered and client-specific. It is not public
self-checkout by default.

## Revenue Control System™ — $1,000/month

**May include**
- Ongoing visibility into revenue, cash, pipeline, and blockers
- Structured check-ins or review cadence if included
- Performance tracking and system health monitoring
- Next-step recommendations and decision support
- Continued clarity after diagnostic and implementation

**Does not mean**
- Unlimited consulting or 24/7 availability
- Emergency business management
- Guaranteed results
- Execution of every task on the owner's behalf
- Replacing the owner's responsibility for decisions
- Accountant, bookkeeper, legal, or tax advisor role

RCS exists because decisions get easier when the right information is
already in front of the owner.

## Add-ons and Custom / Manual Offers

Admin-created, private by default, scoped in writing. Used for:
- Additional product/service or revenue-line diagnostic
- Additional location review
- Extra implementation guidance
- Out-of-scope revisions
- Custom advisory blocks
- Connector setup support
- Special cases

## Checkout & Payment Expectations

- Successful payment creates `paid_pending_access`. It does not unlock the
  full portal.
- RGS reviews the intake before sending the secure portal invite.
- Portal access is invite-only. There is no open public account creation.
- After payment, the next step is intake review and portal invite.
- Reports appear only after RGS publishes them.

## Portal Onboarding (Client-Facing)

New clients should see what happens next, what RGS needs from them, where
to complete diagnostic information, and where reports will appear. Draft
or internal notes never appear in the client portal. Tools are assigned
based on scope and phase — payment does not unlock every tool.

## Admin First-Client Delivery Checklist

After diagnostic payment:
1. Review intake.
2. Check fit and scope; check duplicate-customer warnings.
3. Approve and send invite.
4. Confirm client portal account creation.
5. Confirm diagnostic scope (one primary product/service unless agreed).
6. Assign only the correct tools.
7. Collect needed supporting information.
8. Complete diagnostic review.
9. Draft report; review internal notes.
10. Publish client-safe report.
11. Schedule or send walkthrough if included.
12. Offer Implementation only when appropriate.
13. Offer Revenue Control System™ only when appropriate.

## Acknowledgements Captured at Intake

1. "I understand RGS provides diagnostic, advisory, and system-structure
   guidance. RGS does not guarantee revenue or business outcomes and does
   not provide legal, tax, accounting, or financial advice."
2. "I understand the Business Stability Diagnostic covers one primary
   product, service, or revenue path unless additional scope is agreed in
   writing."

Both are stored on `diagnostic_intakes` (`ack_no_guarantee`,
`ack_one_primary_scope`, `ack_recorded_at`) and required before checkout.

## Fit Guidance

**Good fit** — established owner-led business, active revenue, owner
involved in decisions, wants system clarity, willing to look at evidence,
wants structure and visibility.

**Not a fit** — idea stage only, no consistent revenue yet, wants cheap
execution-only tasks, wants someone to simply run ads or post content,
expects guaranteed results, refuses scope or no-guarantee terms.

If not a fit: be polite, do not shame, suggest the free Scorecard or
general direction, do not push the Diagnostic.

## Boundary Disclaimers (Plain-Spoken)

- RGS does not guarantee revenue, growth, or business outcomes.
- RGS does not provide legal, tax, accounting, HR, or financial advice.
- The owner keeps final decision authority and remains responsible for
  execution.

These statements appear on the public Diagnostic page, the
Implementation page, the Revenue Control System™ page, and inside the
intake acknowledgements.
