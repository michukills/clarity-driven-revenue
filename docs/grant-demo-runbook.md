# RGS Grant Demo Runbook

Audience: grant reviewers and early stakeholders.
Goal: demo RGS clearly in **7–10 minutes** without overclaiming, getting lost in features, or implying client outcomes that don't exist yet.

Tone: confident, plain-English, founder-led, trust-first. No hype. No fake proof. Stay narrow on trade and field service businesses.

---

## 1. Opening — 30-second positioning

> "RGS is an evidence-based revenue diagnostic system for owner-led trade and field service businesses. We help owners find where revenue is being lost across leads, sales, operations, financial visibility, and owner-dependence — and turn that diagnosis into a practical implementation plan. The system is designed to separate what an owner reports from what the evidence actually supports, so recommendations are grounded, not guessed."

Key beats: **trade/service**, **evidence-based**, **diagnostic → implementation**, **trust over hype**.

---

## 2. Public homepage talking points

Open the public homepage (`/`).

- **Hero**: "Evidence-based revenue diagnostics for trade and service businesses" — read it once, don't oversell.
- **Trust note under CTAs**: call attention to it. "The Scorecard is preliminary and self-reported. Final recommendations require diagnostic review and evidence validation." This sets the tone for everything else.
- **Who this is for**: 6 fit-pattern cards. Note this is intentionally narrow — quote-based, field service, owner-led, local service, revenue visibility gaps, growth without systems.
- **What RGS diagnoses**: 5 pillars — Demand Generation, Revenue Conversion, Operational Efficiency, Financial Visibility, Owner Independence.
- **How the diagnostic works**: 7-step flow ending in admin validation before anything is presented as client-facing.
- **"Built to avoid guesswork" trust layer**: this is the thesis of the company. Linger here for ~20 seconds.
- **Footer disclaimer**: scorecard is preliminary, not financial/legal/tax advice.

---

## 3. Scorecard talking points

Open `/scorecard`.

- "This is the front door for an owner who wants a quick read."
- Plain-language questions, ~2 minutes to complete.
- Output is a **preliminary 0–1,000 estimate** with pillar bands.
- **Say explicitly**: "This is a starting signal, not a final diagnosis. RGS would validate this against evidence before recommending action."
- Skip filling it out live unless asked — show the empty form and the trust framing.

---

## 4. Admin OS talking points

Open the admin portal. Stay high-level.

- **Customer lifecycle board**: lead → diagnostic → implementation → ongoing support. Cards move across lanes; package and lifecycle are separate.
- **Diagnostic Workspace**: case file, intake review, connected-source review, imports, uploads, sub-tool status, saved findings, report readiness.
- **Implementation Workspace**: rollout, SOPs, tasks, templates, files, tool distribution.
- **Revenue Tracker / RCC**: separate add-on, not bundled into Diagnostic or Implementation.
- **Trust boundaries**: client-provided data is staged (`provided` → `pending review` → `accepted` / `needs follow-up`). Owner-reported is kept distinct from validated.
- **Admin-only vs client-safe** is enforced — admin findings never leak to client surfaces.

Don't open every tool. Show the shape.

---

## 5. Showcase account walkthrough

All four accounts are **synthetic showcase data** under `@showcase.rgs.local`. Say that out loud once at the top.

### Atlas Home Services — "the system refuses to overclaim"
- Evidence is thin and owner-reported.
- Truth-Test rubric scores it **structurally complete** but readiness is **Not Ready**.
- Talking point: *"The structure looks good, but the evidence underneath isn't strong enough yet. The system flags that honestly instead of producing a confident-sounding report."*
- This is the **trust proof point** — the system penalises itself when evidence is weak.

### Northstar HVAC — "evidence improves confidence"
- Diagnostic evidence (interview + connected-source signals + uploads) is meaningfully better.
- Truth-Test → **Diagnostic-ready / Premium-ready**.
- Talking point: *"Same diagnostic shape, but with real evidence behind it. Confidence goes up because the inputs got better, not because we said so."*

### Summit Roofing & Restoration — "implementation-ready"
- Evidence is strong enough to cross the commercial bar for implementation.
- Findings are sized, prioritized, and tied to evidence refs.
- Talking point: *"At this evidence level, the report is ready to drive an implementation plan, not just a conversation."*

### Keystone Plumbing Co. — "learning over time / RCC outcome story"
- Three drafts: **Week 1, Week 4, Week 8**, plus 8 weekly check-ins.
- Shows accepted recommendations, a **rejected** recommendation (Week 4 emergency-dispatch SOP — owner pushed back, system recorded it), and **outcomes logged** at Week 8 (AR > 60 days dropped 14% → 11%; owner hours 62 → 48; owner belief of 90% utilization contradicted by measured 72%).
- Talking point: *"This is what the system looks like over a quarter — recommendations get accepted, rejected, or measured. The system learns from what actually happened, not from what was promised."*
- Be careful: **outcomes are synthetic**. Use language like "this is the shape of the learning loop", not "this is a real client result".

## 6. Trust-safety lines (use as needed)

- "These four accounts are synthetic showcase data — they let us demonstrate the shape of the system end-to-end."
- "We don't have a real client outcome to claim yet. The product is built so that when we do, the evidence will support it."
- "Evidence changes confidence. When inputs are weak, the system says so."
- "Nothing reaches a client surface until an admin reviews and validates it."

---

## 7. What NOT to say

- Don't say **"proven"**, **"guaranteed"**, **"case study"**, **"real client"**, **"AI knows exactly"**, or **"guaranteed growth"**.
- Don't imply Atlas/Northstar/Summit/Keystone are real businesses or real outcomes.
- Don't pitch MMJ, cannabis, retail, e-commerce, SaaS, agencies, or creators. RGS is trade/service only right now.
- Don't oversell the OS as "AI software". Sell the **diagnostic outcome**: clearer diagnosis, evidence-based findings, prioritized actions, implementation path.
- Don't promise dashboards or features you're not about to show.
- Don't claim live integrations beyond QuickBooks. Other connected sources are request/setup, not live OAuth sync.
