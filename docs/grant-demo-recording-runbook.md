# RGS Grant Demo — Recording Runbook

Audience: grant reviewers and early stakeholders, watching async.
Goal: a **7–8 minute** screen recording that demonstrates RGS clearly, calmly, and trust-first.
Companion to: `docs/grant-demo-runbook.md` (live demo version).

Tone: founder-led, calm, confident, plain English. No hype. No fake proof. Trade and field service only.

## 2. Narration script by timestamp

### 0:00–0:15 — Opening
> "This is RGS — Revenue and Growth Systems. We're an evidence-based revenue diagnostic system for owner-led trade and field service businesses."

### 0:15–0:45 — Public homepage
> "The homepage is intentionally narrow. We diagnose where revenue is being lost across leads, sales, operations, financial visibility, and owner-dependence. The trust note under the CTAs sets the tone for the whole product: the scorecard is preliminary and self-reported, and final recommendations require diagnostic review and evidence validation. That distinction — between what an owner reports and what the evidence supports — is the thesis of the company."

*(Scroll slowly through fit patterns and 5 pillars while talking. Don't read every card.)*

### 0:45–1:45 — Scorecard
> "This is the front door. An owner can complete it in about two minutes and gets a preliminary 0–1,000 estimate with pillar bands. We're explicit that this is a starting signal, not a final diagnosis. RGS validates it against evidence before recommending action. I'm not going to fill it out — I want to show that the trust framing is built into the surface itself."

*(Show the empty form. Don't submit.)*

### 1:45–2:45 — Admin OS
> "Behind the scenes, this is the operating system. Customers move across a lifecycle board — lead, diagnostic, implementation, ongoing support. The Diagnostic Workspace holds the case file, intake review, connected-source review, imports, uploads, sub-tool status, and report readiness. The Implementation Workspace is separate, and the Revenue Tracker is a separate add-on, not bundled. Client-provided data is staged — provided, pending review, accepted, or needs follow-up — so owner-reported is always kept distinct from validated."

*(Hover, don't click into every tool. Show the shape.)*

### 2:45–3:45 — Atlas Home Services
> "These next four accounts are synthetic showcase data. They let us demonstrate the shape of the system end-to-end without claiming a real client outcome."
>
> "Atlas is the trust proof point. The diagnostic structure is complete, but the evidence underneath is thin and mostly owner-reported. The Truth-Test rubric reflects that — structurally complete, but readiness is Not Ready. The system penalises itself when the evidence isn't strong enough. It refuses to produce a confident-sounding report on weak inputs."

### 3:45–4:45 — Northstar HVAC
> "Northstar is the same diagnostic shape, but with meaningfully better evidence — interview answers, connected-source signals, uploads. The Truth-Test moves to Diagnostic-ready. Confidence goes up because the inputs got better, not because we said so."

### 4:45–5:45 — Summit Roofing & Restoration
> "Summit crosses the bar for implementation. Findings are sized, prioritized, and tied to evidence references. At this evidence level, the report is ready to drive an implementation plan, not just a conversation."

### 5:45–7:15 — Keystone Plumbing Co.
> "Keystone shows what the system looks like over a quarter. Three drafts — Week 1, Week 4, Week 8 — and eight weekly check-ins. You can see recommendations that were accepted, one that was rejected when the owner pushed back, and outcomes logged at Week 8. AR over 60 days moved from 14% to 11%. Owner hours moved from 62 to 48. The owner believed crew utilization was 90%; the measured number was 72%, and the system recorded that contradiction."
>
> "These outcomes are synthetic — this is the shape of the learning loop, not a real client result. The point is the system learns from what actually happened, not from what was promised."

### 7:15–7:45 — Close
> "RGS is built so that when we say a business has a revenue problem, the evidence backs it. The showcase data demonstrates the system end-to-end. The next milestone is the first real client run — and the system is ready for it."

*(Hold the final frame for 2 seconds before stopping the recording.)*

## 3. What to show vs. what to skip

**Show**
- Hero, trust note, fit patterns, 5 pillars on the homepage.
- Scorecard form + trust framing (empty).
- Admin lifecycle board.
- One report draft per showcase account.
- Truth-Test readiness state on Atlas and Northstar.
- Prioritized findings on Summit.
- Week 8 view on Keystone with accepted / rejected / outcomes.

**Skip**
- Filling out the scorecard.
- Opening every admin tool.
- Settings, billing, integrations setup screens.
- Edge functions, schema, or anything technical.
- Any tool that isn't directly part of the diagnostic → implementation story.

---

## 4. Trust-safe language

Use these phrasings. They are the safe shape of every claim:
- "Evidence-based revenue diagnostic for trade and field service businesses."
- "These four accounts are synthetic showcase data."
- "We don't have a real client outcome to claim yet."
- "Evidence changes confidence. When inputs are weak, the system says so."
- "Nothing reaches a client surface until an admin reviews and validates it."
- "The scorecard is a starting signal, not a final diagnosis."
- "The system is ready for the first real client run."

---

## 5. What not to say

Do **not** say:
- "Proven", "guaranteed", "guaranteed growth", "guaranteed revenue".
- "Case study", "real client", "real client outcome", "this client".
- "AI knows", "AI knows exactly", "the AI figured out".
- "This replaces the operator", "fully automated".
- Anything implying outcomes that haven't happened.

Do **not** mention:
- MMJ, cannabis, dispensary.
- Retail, retail expansion, e-commerce, SaaS, agencies, creators.
- Industries outside trade and field service.
- Live integrations beyond QuickBooks. Other connected sources are request/setup, not live OAuth sync.
- Roadmap features that aren't on screen.

If you slip and say one of these, stop, take a breath, and re-record from the previous section break. Don't ship a take with forbidden language in it.

## 7. Story beats per showcase account

### Atlas Home Services — "the system refuses to overclaim"
- Beat: evidence is thin and owner-reported.
- Visual: Truth-Test → structurally complete, readiness **Not Ready**.
- Line: *"The structure looks good, but the evidence underneath isn't strong enough yet. The system flags that honestly."*
- Purpose: trust proof point.

### Northstar HVAC — "evidence improves confidence"
- Beat: better interview, connected-source signals, uploads.
- Visual: Truth-Test → **Diagnostic-ready**.
- Line: *"Same diagnostic shape, stronger evidence. Confidence goes up because the inputs got better."*
- Purpose: shows the system rewards real evidence.

### Summit Roofing & Restoration — "implementation-ready"
- Beat: findings sized, prioritized, tied to evidence refs.
- Visual: latest report draft, prioritized findings.
- Line: *"At this evidence level, the report can drive an implementation plan, not just a conversation."*
- Purpose: shows the diagnostic → implementation handoff.

### Keystone Plumbing Co. — "learning over time"
- Beat: Week 1 → Week 4 → Week 8. Accepted recs, one rejected rec, outcomes logged.
- Visual: Week 8 draft. AR > 60 days 14% → 11%. Owner hours 62 → 48. Owner-belief 90% utilization vs measured 72%.
- Line: *"This is the shape of the learning loop. The system learns from what actually happened, not from what was promised."*
- Purpose: shows the quarter-long arc and the owner-belief vs measured contradiction.
- **Trust guard**: outcomes are synthetic. Say it.
